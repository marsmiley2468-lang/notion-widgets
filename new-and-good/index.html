CCC Seeks Proposals for Special Issue: 'Writing Pedagogy as Intellectual Fun, Emergence, and Joy'
College Composition & Communication is accepting proposals for its September 2027 special issue, framing intellectual fun as a legitimate orientation toward writing pedagogy — one that invites joy, delight, and experimentation alongside serious inquiry. Proposals were due June 5, 2026, making this a live CFP for R&C scholars. It's a meaningful signal that the field is actively reclaiming pleasure and play as scholarly values, not just buzzwords.

Bellingham Celebrates Fourth Annual Juneteenth Gathering on Commercial Street
Hundreds of community members gathered on Commercial Street in Bellingham for the fourth annual Juneteenth celebration, featuring performances by local up-and-coming artists. The event marks another year of the community showing up to honor and celebrate Black freedom and history. Juneteenth is also a reminder of the deep connection between freedom and the fight for literacy — in 1865, about 90% of Black Southerners were illiterate due to anti-literacy laws, a number that dropped sharply in the fifteen years that followed.

France Protected 387,000 Acres of Forest in a Single Move Toward Its 2030 Conservation Goal
On June 9, France created seven new biological reserves and expanded two existing ones, adding over 387,000 acres of protected land — the largest being a tropical rainforest and granite peaks reserve in French Guiana. The move brings France closer to its goal of placing 10% of its land under strong protection by 2030. Protected reserves are proven to prevent species extinction, store carbon, and buffer communities from flooding and erosion.
