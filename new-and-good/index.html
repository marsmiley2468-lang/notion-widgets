Scientists Identify 64,000 Square Miles of Coral Reef That Can Survive Climate Change
A new analysis of 45,000 coral surveys found nearly 64,000 square miles of climate-resilient coral reef — three times more than previous estimates — spanning 71 countries. Researchers say the reefs identified can both survive and recover from climate change. One author noted that people tend to see coral as 'beyond saving,' but this research 'proves otherwise.' The findings will help governments prioritize protections toward the global 30-by-30 conservation goal.
