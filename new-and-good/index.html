CCC calls for papers on "Writing Pedagogy as Intellectual Fun, Emergence, and Joy"
College Composition & Communication is soliciting proposals for a 2027 special issue reframing intellectual fun as an orientation toward writing pedagogy — one that invites joy, delight, and experimentation alongside sustained inquiry. It's a rare CFP that treats pleasure as a serious methodological stance rather than a bonus. Worth a look if the multimodal and AI-in-the-classroom work you're doing wants a venue.

Bellingham's Way Station has served nearly 1,400 people since opening
The downtown hygiene and health center has roughly doubled its original projections since opening in November 2024, offering showers, laundry, and basic care to Whatcom County's unhoused population. In May it partnered with the Opportunity Council and PeaceHealth to open a Respite Center for recently discharged hospital patients without a safe place to recover.

Malaria vaccine developer wins the European Inventor Award 2026
Sir Adrian Hill and his Oxford team took the prize for the R21/Matrix-M vaccine, which showed roughly 75–80% protection against malaria in clinical trials. Around 20 countries have now rolled it out, likely preventing tens of thousands of deaths. A reminder that some public-health work still moves fast and lands.
