Kairos Spring 2026 Issue Explores Generative AI and Multimodal Composition
Kairos: A Journal of Rhetoric, Technology, and Pedagogy has published its Spring 2026 issue (30.2), featuring work that juxtaposes games created with generative AI to interrogate how these tools function in humanities contexts. The issue reflects growing scholarly interest in multimodal composition and the rhetorical implications of AI-generated content — directly relevant to current debates in writing pedagogy.

Bellingham's Bike Patrol Officers Are Changing How the City Polices Downtown
Cascadia Daily News rode along with Bellingham's five-officer downtown bike patrol unit, reinstated in early 2025 after a decade-long hiatus. Officers know residents by name, prioritize connection over punitive action, and have earned unequivocal support from downtown businesses. Lt. Claudia Murphy says the change has been 'positive on so many levels' — and bikes are here to stay.

Portland Nonprofit Opens Free 30,000-Square-Foot Grocery Store for Families in Need
Sunshine Division, a Portland nonprofit, opened a free grocery store in a 30,000-square-foot facility designed to give families access to fresh, quality food — no income verification required. The store expects to serve 100,000 households this year, a significant expansion of food security in the Pacific Northwest.
