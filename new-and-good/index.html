Skagit County Wetlands Are Being Restored After 19th-Century Drainage
Cascadia Daily News reports on active wetland restoration projects in Skagit County, where settlers drained the delta in the 1800s to create farmland — destroying native ecosystems in the process. Conservationists are now working to bring marshlands back, with both ecological and community benefits. A story of repair happening in your own backyard.

Free Grocery Store in Portland Will Serve 100,000 Families This Year
Sunshine Division, a Portland nonprofit, opened a 30,000-square-foot free grocery store where families can shop for themselves — choosing items that fit their needs, allergies, and dietary preferences rather than accepting pre-packed boxes. About 80% of inventory comes from local farms, retailers, and food partners, and the store expects to serve 100,000 households in year one.
