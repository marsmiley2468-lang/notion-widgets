CCC Seeks Essays on Writing Pedagogy as Joy and Intellectual Fun
College Composition and Communication is building a 2027 special issue around 'Writing Pedagogy as Intellectual Fun, Emergence, and Joy.' Editors want work exploring what becomes possible when pedagogy is approached as a site of delight, experimentation, and fellow-feeling — particularly pieces that revisit or invent new pedagogies for the current moment. Genres include articles, research briefs, review essays, and symposia.

Whatcom County Enacts One-Year Ban on Immigration Detention Centers
The Whatcom County Council voted to temporarily ban new immigration detention centers, passing a one-year moratorium while the county clarifies its land use code. The move gives local officials time to formally define whether and how such facilities can be sited in Whatcom County — a significant local civic decision that reflects broader community concerns about federal detention infrastructure near Bellingham.

Portland Nonprofit Opens Free Grocery Store for 100,000 Families
Sunshine Division opened a 30,000-square-foot free grocery store in northwest Portland where families shop for themselves — choosing items that fit their diet, allergies, and preferences rather than receiving prepackaged boxes. About 80% of inventory comes from local grocers, farms, and retailers. The store models a dignified, choice-centered approach to food access and expects to serve 100,000 households in its first year.
